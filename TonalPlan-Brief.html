TonalPlan

A weight converter and AI-style workout planner for the Tonal home gym.

Overview

TonalPlan is a web app for people who train on a Tonal. It solves two problems: Tonal's digital resistance feels heavier than the same number in free weights, and building effective, equipment-appropriate workouts is tedious. TonalPlan converts weights both ways, learns each user's personal conversion from their own logged lifts, and generates complete, Tonal-native workouts from a plain-language goal — drawing on the full 315-movement Tonal catalog.

What it does

  • Weight converter. Converts Tonal digital weight ↔ free-weight equivalent in both directions, using a research-based formula or the user's own data when available.
  • My Lifts. Log what a Tonal weight felt equivalent to in free weights; the app averages entries into a personal multiplier per exercise that sharpens future conversions.
  • Goal-driven Workout Creator. Describe a goal (“more distance on my golf drive,” “stronger chest”) and get a full warmup → blocks → finisher → cooldown session using only moves your equipment supports, with suggested starting weights from your own history.
  • Movement Library. The complete 315-move Tonal catalog, searchable by name, muscle, movement pattern, or accessory, with an “only my equipment” filter.
  • Edit & personalize. Rename workouts, edit descriptions, and swap any movement for a similar-muscle alternative; each generation varies so plans stay fresh.
  • Cross-device accounts. Email/password sign-in with data synced across phone and laptop, and a live sync indicator.
  • Export to Tonal. “Copy for Tonal” produces a clean build sheet to recreate any workout in Tonal's own Custom Workouts feature.

How it works

The workout engine never invents exercises: it filters the curated movement database to the user's owned accessories, scores each candidate against the words in their goal (muscles, patterns, and training intent), and assembles a balanced session — capping repetition so a workout isn't three near-identical presses. Personal conversion data feeds both the converter and the suggested loads.
