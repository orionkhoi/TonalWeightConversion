# TonalPlan — The Dream Version

> Dream-mode notes from an interview. No feasibility here. This is what "perfect" looks like before anything gets cut.

## The one-line shift

Today TonalPlan is a tool you reach for. The dream version is a coach that lives where your data lives — and it starts the conversation.

## What perfect looks like

**It talks first.** The coach messages you before you open anything. "Skip what you had planned — your lifts have been dragging all week. Here's 20 minutes of mobility instead." You reach for it less because it's already there.

**It persuades — it doesn't just inform.** When it changes your plan, it gives you a reason, and the reason is built to move you, not just to notify you. It expects you to resist, and it argues accordingly: "You've bailed on legs three Mondays straight. I'm putting them first today before you talk yourself out of it."

**It learns which lever works on you.** Some people move for pride, some for guilt, some for a streak, some for a photo. The coach watches which messages actually get you off the couch and gets better at reaching you over time. Motivation is personalized, not generic.

**It rewards honesty over perfection.** The streak was rewarding the appearance of consistency — which is exactly why people fake injuries to protect it. The dream version flips that: the day you say "I'm hurt" or "I just don't have it today," that honesty is the winning move, not the losing one. "Telling me that is the workout today. Real recovery, no penalty." A streak that survives a skipped day is worth more than one that punishes the truth.

**It acknowledges the bad day.** When your lifts signal you're off, it doesn't go silent and it doesn't get tough — it names it and carries you through. "Today's not your day, so let's just move a little. Showing up is the win." The acknowledgment is what gets you through it.

**It can see you.** Progress photos aren't a gallery — they're the coach's eyes. It catches the plateau your mirror hides, notices imbalances before they become pain ("your left shoulder sits lower — let's fix it"), and on the worst day it skips the argument and just shows you your own before-and-after. No sentence beats *look how far you've come*.

**It becomes the right coach for each person.** Same engine, different bedside manner. For you: warm, encouraging, celebrates small wins. For someone who needs a drill sergeant: sharper. The coach has range, and it learns each user's dialect of motivation.

## The design principles you found by feel

**Use what's already in front of it — don't reach.** The signals you kept (lift data, screen time, replies to the system) all live inside the app's own turf. The ones you cut (sleep, calendar) needed outside gear or felt like snooping. Principle: lean on first-party, low-creep data first.

**Lift data is the crown jewel.** Always available, requires no extra hardware, and nobody finds it creepy that a workout app watches your workouts. It's the most honest read on how you're really doing.

**Positive — but never dishonest.** You need encouragement, not brutal honesty. But pure positivity that lies erodes trust. The move is to reframe, not to flatter: truth, delivered warm. "Strength dipped, but you trained through a brutal week and still showed up. That's the win — now let's rebuild."

## Open questions to carry into spec

- **A/B-ing the user.** If it learns which motivation lever works by testing them, how much of that experimentation is fair game before it feels like being played?
- **When to release you.** A coach who only ever pushes is a bad coach. How does it know the day you're right to skip — and hand you permission that actually means something?
- **The receipts question.** Should it be allowed to quote your own past goals back at you when you're being lazy? Powerful, but where's the line into resentment?
- **Mood vs. trust on the worst day.** When the news is genuinely bad, which does it protect? That single choice is the whole personality of the product.
- **The streak's fans.** Killing the punishing streak rewards honesty — but some users need that dumb little number. What replaces it for them?

## Next step

When you're ready to leave dream mode: pick the 2–3 pieces here that are both the most magic and the most "you," and those become the spine of the summer flagship.
